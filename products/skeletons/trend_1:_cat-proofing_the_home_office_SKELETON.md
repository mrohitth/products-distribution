# Product Skeleton: Cat-Proofing the Home Office

---

## 1. Product Title

**Cat-Proof Your Office Without Losing Your Mind**

*(40 characters — validates the frustration, makes no false promises, uses the actual language someone would mutter at 2pm on a Tuesday)*

---

## 2. Emotional Hook

"Every time I close my laptop, she's fine. The second I open Slack — there she is. Walking directly across my keyboard. Like she knows."

Sound familiar?

You're not bad at your job. You're not bad at owning a cat. You're just working from the same physical space as a small predator who has zero concept of quarterly deadlines. Cats don't sit on your keyboard because they hate you. They sit there because your keyboard is warm, interesting, and right in the middle of the thing you're clearly paying a lot of attention to. You're not fighting your cat. You're fighting your cat's instincts — and you've been losing that fight alone.

This isn't about accepting chaos or "just closing the door." It's about building a workspace that works for two species who were never supposed to share a home office. We're going to diagnose the specific triggers driving your cat's worst habits, restructure your physical setup so curiosity doesn't equal destruction, and give you a system that holds up when your cat inevitably adapts and escalates.

No "just Zen about it" advice. No toys you bought that sit untouched. A real setup, for a real situation.

---

## 3. Product Architecture

**Part 1: The Cat Brain Breakdown** *(Why Nothing You've Tried Has Worked)*
Chapters cover the real reasons cats target keyboards, cables, and screens — and why "just distract them" falls apart under real work conditions. You'll learn to see your office from your cat's incentive structure, which changes everything.

**Part 2: The Workspace Audit** *(Find Your Cat's Favorite Crime Scene)*
A diagnostic walkthrough that identifies exactly which zones trigger your cat's worst behavior. Most people try to solve the problem everywhere. You only have to fix four or five specific spots.

**Part 3: The Setup** *(Physical Architecture + Environmental Engineering)*
Physical modifications (cable management, targeted barriers, workstation geometry) paired with environmental triggers (scent, texture, sound zones) that redirect your cat's attention before your focus is destroyed.

**Part 4: The Adaptation Protocol** *(When Your Cat Changes the Game)*
Cats learn. Your setup will eventually stop working. This section gives you a framework for staying ahead — with a maintenance cadence so you're not rebuilding from scratch every six weeks.

---

## 4. Key Human Touch Elements

- **The "it's not you, it's them" reframe.** Cat-proofing failure isn't a discipline problem — it's a setup problem. This framing alone reduces shame and increases compliance.
- **The "deadline interruption" specific.** This product speaks to a precise scenario: you're on a timer, your cat has chosen this exact moment to climb onto your monitor. That's not generic pet advice. That's your Tuesday.
- **Acknowledging the guilt.** The solution cannot require you to lock your cat away or feel like a bad owner. Every recommendation respects the relationship. This is critical — cat owners feel real guilt about "restricting" their pet, even when restriction is the right call.

---

## 5. Format & Pricing

**Format:** PDF guide (structured for fast reference during setup) + companion worksheet with the workspace audit checklist + audio summary for commute listening.

**Price point:** $27 — $37 range. The work-from-home market has already been conditioned to spend $20–40 on productivity tools. Framing this as "career infrastructure protection" (not "cat tips") allows premium positioning without resistance. It's not a pet guide. It's a setup that preserves your income.

---

## 6. Distribution

- **r/remodeling + r/homeoffice + r/catadvice** — Reddit threads asking "how do I stop my cat from destroying my desk setup" are constant. First comment shares the guide. Seed, don't spam.
- **Product Hunt** — Launch in Q1. Work-from-home tools get strong traffic there. The framing is "office setup for remote workers with cats." Not cutesy.
- **LinkedIn newsletter shares** — Remote work influencers often mention home office setup. Pitch the infrastructure angle: "I protected my own work environment — here's the framework."
- **Cat-adjacent newsletters** — Not pet newsletters. Office/lifestyle publications with cat-owner audiences. The specificity of the audience matters more than volume.

---

## 7. Market Intel: Human Infrastructure Angle

The "human infrastructure" thesis says people are spending real money to protect the conditions of their productivity — not just the tools, but the environment around the tools. Ergonomic chairs. Noise-canceling headphones. Lighting. Blue light glasses.

Cat-proofing the home office is the same investment, one layer deeper. You're not buying comfort. You're buying back 30–45 minutes of focus per day that currently evaporates to cat-induced interruptions. That has a real hourly value. That makes this a productivity purchase dressed in cat content — which means the market is larger than "cat owners" and the pricing ceiling is higher than "pet guide."
