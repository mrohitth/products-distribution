# Product Skeleton: *"The Homeowner Exit: A Decision System for Forced Relocation"*

> **Note on title:** "Homeowner Exit Strategy" is direct and implies urgency. It speaks to the reader who has a deadline (job relocation, life change) and needs a decision, not just information. The word "exit" signals they're already committed to leaving — they just need the path.

---

## 1. Emotional Hook (The First 400 Words)

This product opens with the specific pain of being trapped: house won't sell, deadline is real, family is stressed.

**Structure:**
- Open with an anonymous adaptation of the Reddit post: *"You listed your house in May at what your realtor said was a fair price. You've had 18 showings. Three offers, all 30-40k under asking, with repair lists a mile long. You've dropped the price twice and you're still stuck. And now you need to be in Orlando by the end of next month for your wife's new job. You're not just selling a house. You're watching a timeline collapse."*
- Immediate validation: *"You're not in a unique situation — the market shifted, your house is in a condition that triggers lowball offers, and the clock is louder than you expected. But you have more options than you think."*
- The pivot: *"This isn't a real estate guide. It's a decision system. By the end of this, you'll know exactly which exit path to take: realtor, iBuyer, cash buyer, lease-to-own, or a hybrid. And you'll know why, with the math to back it up."*

**Tone rule:** Calm under pressure. The reader is stressed — the product needs to be the calm in the storm. No cheerleading, no false optimism. Just clear analysis and a path forward.

---

## 2. Part 1: The Five Exit Paths (The Decision Framework)

**Core insight:** Most homeowners in this situation do what the Reddit poster did: go to the internet and ask strangers "what should I do?" They get 50 opinions and no clarity. This section cuts through the noise with a structured comparison.

**Sections:**
- **The Path Comparison Matrix** — A table comparing all five exit paths on: timeline (fastest to slowest), net proceeds (what you'll actually walk away with after fees), control (how much say you have in the process), risk (what can go wrong). Five paths, one comparison grid.
- **Path 1: Traditional Realtor Sale** — When it makes sense, when it doesn't, what the numbers actually look like (6% commission, closing costs, repair credits, 60-90 day timeline). The case study: the Reddit poster's situation — is this a good fit for them?
- **Path 2: iBuyer (Opendoor, Offerpad, Zillow Offers)** — When it makes sense, when it doesn't, fees (typically 5-9%), speed (7-14 days), the "convenience premium" math. For a Tampa house in this condition, what's the iBuyer offer likely to look like?
- **Path 3: Cash Buyer (Investor / We Buy Houses)** — When it makes sense, when it doesn't, how to find legitimate cash buyers vs. scams, the math (typically 60-80% of market value), the speed (5-14 days).
- **Path 4: Lease-to-Own / Seller Financing** — When it makes sense, when it doesn't, how to structure it to protect yourself, the tax implications, the timeline (90-180 days typically).
- **Path 5: The Hybrid — Agent + Price Adjustment + Concessions** — The middle path: reduce price further, offer closing cost credits, get a fresh appraisal, try again with better positioning.

**Human touch element:** *"Here's the uncomfortable math: if you sell to a cash buyer at 70% of market value on a $399k listing, you might walk away with $270k after fees. If you hold on for 60 more days, do minor repairs, and sell at $370k with a realtor, you might walk away with $335k. But you need that 60-day window — and you don't have it. The question isn't 'which path gives me the most money.' It's 'which path gives me enough money in the time I have.'"*

## 3. Part 2: The Timeline Pressure Test (Are You Actually Stuck?)

**Core insight:** The Reddit poster has a hard deadline: end of next month. That's about 30 days. This section forces the reader to confront the timeline and evaluate which paths are actually on the table given their constraints.

**Sections:**
- **The 30-Day Test** — If you have 30 days, which paths are viable? Which are not? The answer: iBuyer and cash buyer are the only real 30-day options. Traditional sale is not viable in 30 days. Lease-to-own is not viable in 30 days.
- **The Hard Deadline Conversation** — How to have the conversation with your spouse about what's actually possible in the time remaining. Script: "Here's what we can do in 30 days. Here's what we can't. Which tradeoffs are we willing to make?"
- **The "Extend the Deadline" Options** — What does it actually take to push the Orlando deadline by 30-60 days? Is that feasible? What's the cost of delay?
- **The Emergency Exit Option** — If all else fails, what's the worst-case scenario? (Rent the house, take a loss, move to Orlando, deal with it remotely.) This isn't defeatism — it's a pressure release valve.

**Human touch element:** *"I'm not going to tell you 'don't panic.' You have a right to panic. What I'm going to tell you is what your actual options are given the time you have, and which ones are fantasy. In 30 days, you have two real options. Let's figure out which one works."*

## 4. Part 3: The Math Deep Dive (For Each Path, The Actual Numbers)

**Core insight:** Most homeowners don't know the real math behind selling a house. They think "list price minus mortgage payoff" is the equation. It's not. This section breaks down every fee, every cost, every concession.

**Sections:**
- **The Full Cost of Sale Worksheet** — Mortgage payoff, agent commissions (both buyer and seller sides), closing costs (typically 2-5% of sale price), transfer taxes, prorated property taxes, any liens or HELOC payoffs, repair credits and concessions, home warranty costs, moving costs. A complete list so the reader knows what they're actually comparing.
- **The Net Proceeds Calculator** — A fillable worksheet that takes the list price, applies each cost, and gives a real net proceeds number. This is the number that matters — not the list price.
- **For Each Path: The Net Proceeds Estimate** — Traditional realtor: $399k → net ~$358k after all fees. iBuyer: $340-360k (after 5-9% fee) → net ~$310-325k. Cash buyer: $280-320k → net ~$265-305k. The reader needs to see these side by side to make a real decision.
- **The "What If I Fix It" Calculator** — If they spend $5k on repairs, what's the likely price increase? If they spend $10k? The math on whether repair investment is worth it given the timeline.

**Human touch element:** *"You will not believe how many people tell me 'I want to sell my house for $400k' without knowing they'll walk away with $355k. The list price is fiction. The net proceeds are reality. This worksheet makes it real."*

## 5. Part 4: The Execution Plan (The 7-Day Action Sequence)

**Core insight:** Decision paralysis is the biggest risk. The reader needs a 7-day action plan that moves them from "stuck" to "contract signed."

**Sections:**
- **Day 1-2: Get Real Numbers** — Request iBuyer offers (all three major platforms), call two cash buyer companies, get a comparative market analysis from a new realtor. Don't commit to anything. Just gather data.
- **Day 3-4: Run the Numbers** — Use the Net Proceeds Calculator on each option. Compare. Decide which 2-3 paths are actually viable given the timeline.
- **Day 5-6: Make the Call** — Contact the top path, negotiate terms, request a contract. For cash buyers: request proof of funds. For iBuyers: confirm the all-cash guarantee. For realtors: negotiate commission or concessions.
- **Day 7: Sign and Move Forward** — Sign the contract, notify the other parties (you're committed now, so the pressure is off), start scheduling the move.

**Human touch element:** *"The moment you sign a contract, something shifts. The uncertainty ends. The timeline becomes real. You can stop reading Reddit threads at 2am asking strangers what to do. You have a path. This guide is designed to get you to that contract in 7 days."*
