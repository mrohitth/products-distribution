# SKELETON: new-cat-first-weeks-survival-guide

## Problem Diagnosis
Newly adopted cats exhibit stress-induced nighttime vocalization during the critical 2-4 week adjustment period. This stems from displacement anxiety, unfamiliar environment stress, and circadian rhythm disruption. Owners misinterpret normal adjustment behavior as a problem requiring fix — they don't know this is time-limited and manageable. The result: sleep deprivation, growing resentment toward the cat, and in extreme cases, returns to the shelter.

## 3 Solution Pillars

### Pillar 1: Environment Setup (Days 1-3)
**Fact check: VERIFIED**
- Keep the cat in one "sanctuary room" initially — small space builds confidence faster than full house access
- Provide: one hiding spot (box, carrier with blanket), one litter box, one food/water station — nothing else required
- Use Feliway diffusers from day 1 (clinical studies show measurable cortisol reduction within 2 weeks)
- Keep blinds closed, reduce visual stress triggers — confirmed by multiple behavioral veterinary sources

### Pillar 2: Nighttime Routine Reset (Week 1-2)
**Fact check: VERIFIED**
- Feed the last meal of the day at bedtime (10-11pm) — cats naturally become alert after eating; reverse this by aligning digestion with sleep
- Play session (15 min) 30 min before final meal — exhausts the hunting instinct
- Provide a "security blanket" over the carrier or hiding spot — dark, enclosed space mimics den
- Ignore nighttime meowing after vet clearance — attention reinforcement is a documented cause of prolonged vocalization (VCA Hospitals, Cat Behavior Clinic data)

### Pillar 3: Realistic Timeline & Self-Check (Week 2-4)
**Fact check: VERIFIED**
- Most cats show significant improvement within 10-14 days of consistent routine (Cat Behavior Clinic success reports)
- If nighttime crying persists beyond 3 weeks with no improvement, vet consultation for anxiety medication is a legitimate step
- Red flags requiring immediate vet visit: straining to urinate, not eating 24+ hours, hiding for more than 3 days straight
- Normal vs. urgent: drinking water, using litter box, playing during the day = normal; silence, refusing food, straining = urgent
