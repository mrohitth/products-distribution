## Product Skeleton: New Pet Owner Survival Guide

---

### 1. Product Title (40 chars max)

**Kitten Recovery Guide** *(22 chars)*
or
**Survive Your First 30 Days** *(25 chars)*

---

### 2. Emotional Hook (~200 words)

*Open with adaptation:*

> "Got my first kitten a week ago. She peed on my bed, attacked my ankles, and screams at 4am. The shelter said she'd be easy. I feel like I'm failing."

That feeling is real, and it is **not** a sign you're a bad pet owner. It's a sign you were handed a dependent creature with zero instruction manual and told "good luck." Nobody tells you that the first two weeks aren't a test of your character — they're a setup phase. Kittens don't come programmed. They come feral.

Here's what the shelters don't say: **the chaos has a predictable shape.** Once you understand why a kitten does what she does at 2am, the behavior stops feeling like an attack and starts feeling like a solvable problem. This guide is that decoder.

Over the next 30 days, you'll set up systems that make her predictable, feed schedules that actually work, and boundaries that don't require you to yell at 4am. We'll cover exactly when a behavior is normal-puppy-chaos versus when it needs a vet. By day 30, you'll have a cat who trusts you, a home that doesn't smell like pee, and zero guilt about bringing her home.

---

### 3. Product Architecture

**PART 1: The First 72 Hours**
Establishes the physical infrastructure (litter setup, safe room, feeding stations) and the mental shift from "pet owner" to "system builder." Chapters: Safe Room Protocol, Reading Body Language, The Feeding Architecture.

**PART 2: Week 1–2 — Behavioral Expectation Calibration**
Identifies which behaviors are species-normal, which are anxiety-driven, and which need immediate correction. Includes a first-week triage worksheet to prioritize interventions. Chapters: Normal vs. Concerning, Litter Box Rescue Plan, Night-Waking Reset Protocol.

**PART 3: Week 3–4 — Integration and Confidence**
Introduces multi-room exploration, introduces other pets if applicable, and establishes a sustainable daily rhythm both of you can live with. Chapters: The Household Integration Map, Sustainable Feeding Rhythm, Socialization Without Overwhelm.

**PART 4: Day 30 Checklist and "When to Call" Guide**
Covers exactly what a healthy, adjusting pet looks like at day 30, red flags that warrant vet calls, and the one-sentence decision framework for "Is this a phase or a problem?" Chapters: The 30-Day Benchmark, Vet-Visit Triage, Your Ongoing Playbook.

---

### 4. Key Human Touch Elements

- **The 4am honesty check**: "Nobody tells you the first week is about the human adjusting, not the pet. If you're crying at 2am, that's the guide working. That's you learning."
- **Naming the guilt**: "You didn't fail. You just started without a map."
- **The "I almost returned him" stories**: Collected from community — normalized, not sensationalized. Shows the reader they're not alone at the exact moment they feel most isolated.
- **Sensory specificity**: Litter box placement near air vents, the specific sound of a stressed meow vs. a demand meow, the exact texture of anxiety-driven chewing. Ground the reader in the physical reality of what's happening.

---

### 5. Format & Pricing

**Format:** PDF + 4 fillable worksheets (first-week triage, 30-day benchmark, behavior log, vet-visit prep template). PDF allows for on-device reading at 3am.

**Price point rationale:** $27–$37. This is below the cost of one vet visit for an anxiety-driven issue (a UTI from stress, a litter aversion that becomes a carpet problem). The price should feel like *insurance*, not a course. At $27, the question isn't "is this worth it" — it's "what's one less takeout order to avoid returning this animal."

**Upsell angle:** Community membership (day-30 check-in threads, vet Q&A) at $47/yr — but only introduced in the PDF's last page.

---

### 6. Distribution

- **Primary:** Reddit (r/CatAdvice, r/petowners, r/NewParents-adjacent communities) — genuine problem-solving posts, not promotion. Comment with a link to the guide after identifying the exact struggle.
- **Secondary:** TikTok/Reels — "things nobody told you about week one with a kitten" with a link in bio. High share rate on the 4am scream content. The video doesn't sell; it identifies. The link converts.
- **Tertiary:** Shelter/rescue partnerships. Every adoption packet sent home should include a QR code to the guide. Shelters *want* people to succeed — they just can't provide follow-up. This fills that gap.

---

### 7. Market Intel: "Human Infrastructure" Angle

People are spending more on pets than ever — not because they have more money, but because they have fewer support systems. Pets are filling an emotional infrastructure gap: companionship without the complexity of human relationships. When that pet has a problem, the owner has *no one* to call. No family member, no coworker who gets it.

The "human infrastructure" thesis holds here: people are quietly building their own support stacks (pet, routine, environment, tools) because institutional support (family, community, institutions) has thinned out. A new pet owner survival guide isn't a niche product — it's infrastructure for a person who has chosen to build a life around a creature that depends on them entirely.

Sell the confidence. Not the information.
