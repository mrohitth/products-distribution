## 2. Part 1: The Pre-Trip Audit (3 Weeks Out)

**Core insight:** Toddlers don't have insomnia — they have schedule disruptions. The "vacation regression" happens because everything changes at once: new room, new sounds, new crib, new schedule, newtimezone. You can't control the new room, but you can control everything else.

**Sections:**
- **The Schedule Stabilization Protocol (Week -3 to -1):** Start adjusting sleep schedule to destination timezone *before* you leave. Move bedtime 15 minutes every 3 days in the direction of the destination timezone. Most parents don't know this is possible.
- **The Crib Continuity Kit:** Bring the sleep sack, the sound machine, the blackout curtain, the lovey. The room changes; these things don't. Photograph the current setup. Recreate it in the rental — same corner orientation if possible, same sound machine decibel level, same white noise track.
- **The "Dry Run" Weekend:** Three weeks out, do a "vacation simulation" at home. Put your toddler in the pack-and-play in a different room, run the white noise at the same volume you'd use in a rental, use the travel blackout curtain. They don't need to know it's practice — you're calibrating the environment, not training them.
- **The Packing Checklist That Actually Works:** Not a generic baby packing list. A sleep-specific checklist: monitor, white noise machine, travel blackout curtain, sleep sack (x2), lovey (backup), outlet converter, portable sound machine app on phone as backup.

## 3. Part 2: The Travel Day Protocol

**Core insight:** The travel day is the highest-risk moment. Overstimulation + schedule disruption + unfamiliar environment = predictable meltdown. You need a protocol, not a hope.

**Sections:**
- **The Day-of Sleep Window:** Keep your toddler in the car seat or stroller during their usual nap window, even if it means skipping a meal. A napping toddler in transit is better than a hungry toddler at a restaurant at 2 PM who then won't sleep at 5 PM.
- **The "Arrival Hour" Rules:** First 60 minutes in the rental: no screens, no sugar, no new people, no extended play. The room is dark, the sound machine is on, the routine starts immediately. Parents who skip this step pay for it for the next three days.
- **The First Nap Blueprint:** Whatever time your toddler usually naps, put them down in the rental room at that time — even if they've been up for "long enough." Don't wait for yawns. Use the clock, not the yawns.
- **The Red-Eye Exception:** Long-haul or red-eye travel requires a different protocol: strategic sleep debt, cabin environment prep, and a "recovery day" built into the itinerary (day 1 is a half-day, not a full day of activities).

## 4. Part 3: The Vacation Sleep Stack (The Core Framework)

**Core insight:** This is the product's centerpiece — a named, repeatable framework that parents can use on every trip. "The Sleep Stack" becomes the brand concept.

**The Sleep Stack — 5 Layers:**

1. **Light Control:** Blackout curtains or travel blackout cling. Room must be dark by 7 PM even if the sun is still up at your destination.
2. **Sound Masking:** White noise machine at the same decibel level used at home. Not a lullaby — white noise. Lullabies刺激, white noise soothes.
3. **Temperature:** Rental houses are often 68–72°F at night. Toddlers sleep best at 68–70°F. Open a window or turn on the AC to hit the target.
4. **Routine Continuity:** Same bath → same pajamas → same book → same song → same sleep sack. The sequence must be identical to home.
5. **Object Continuity:** The lovey, the sleep sack, the sound machine — these travel with you. They are not optional. This is non-negotiable and should be stated firmly.

**The Stack in Practice:** A one-page printable that fits in a diaper bag. Parents photograph it, reference it, use it.

## 5. Part 4: The "Already Melted Down" Recovery Protocol

**Core insight:** Not every parent will read this before their first ruined trip. The product needs to serve the parent who is *in* the rental room at 9 PM, already in crisis.

**Sections:**
- **The Reset Button:** 5 steps to stop the spiral:
  1. Darken the room fully (blackout curtain on, lights off)
  2. Sound machine on full volume for 2 minutes — not to put them to sleep, to reset the auditory environment
  3. Hold, don't speak, for 90 seconds — no words, just presence
  4. Offer water / a feed / a small snack (hunger is often underneath the meltdown)
  5. Start the sleep stack from layer 1
- **The "This Trip Isn't Ruined" Reframe:** Data point: one bad night's sleep on vacation does not create a sleep regression that lasts. The regression happens when parents *react* to the bad night with anxiety and overcorrection the next day. The next day is a new day.
- **The "Recovery Day" Formula:** If you've had 2 bad nights, day 3 needs to be a half-day. Short morning outing, full nap at home base (or in the stroller), early dinner, early bedtime. Don't try to "make up" the itinerary.
