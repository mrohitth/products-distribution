# Quick-Start Checklist: No Shame Saturday + 5 Core Scripts

*Extracted from "You First, For Once" — $7 Order Bump*

---

## PART A: No Shame Saturday — Launch in 3 Steps

### Step 1: Pick Your Block
- [ ] Saturday or Sunday, 8 AM to noon (or any 4-hour block)
- [ ] Mark it on the calendar. It's non-negotiable.
- [ ] Tell your teen: *"This is my time. Unless someone is bleeding or the house is on fire, I'm not available."*

### Step 2: Set the Boundaries
- [ ] Door closed (get a doorstop if you don't have a lock)
- [ ] Phone on Do Not Disturb
- [ ] Breakfast is whatever they can find without the stove
- [ ] No making up the time later — this isn't borrowed, it's maintenance

### Step 3: Do It Anyway
- [ ] Week 1: You'll feel selfish. Do it anyway.
- [ ] Week 2: You'll hear knocking. Ignore it.
- [ ] Week 3: You'll notice you're less reactive.
- [ ] Week 4: Your kid will stop knocking. The new normal is set.

## PART B: 5 Core Scripts — Screenshot These

### 1. The Meltdown Protocol
**Say:** *"You're overwhelmed. That makes sense. We'll circle back after dinner."*

**Don't say:** "Calm down." "You're overreacting."

### 2. The Homework Standoff
**Say:** *"Your grades are your job. I'll help if you ask — before 9 PM."*

**Don't say:** "If you don't do your homework, you'll never get into college."

### 3. The Chore War
**Say:** *"I cooked. You clean. Pick the music."*

**Don't say:** "Because I said so."

### 4. The Screen Time Standoff
**Say:** *"Phone charges in the kitchen at 9:30. Mine too."*

**Don't say:** "You're addicted to that thing."

### 5. The "I Hate My Body" Moment
**Say:** *"I remember that feeling. Tell me more."*

**Don't say:** "You're beautiful just the way you are."

---

## PART C: The 20% Rule — Week 1 Kickoff

- [ ] Sit down with your teen (not during a conflict)
- [ ] Say: *"I'm exhausted. Not 'long day' tired — the kind where I'm starting to resent my own life. I need your help changing how things work. Can we talk about that?"*
- [ ] Together, pick ONE recurring task they'll own starting this week
- [ ] Write it down. Put it on the fridge. Make it visible.

**One task. One week. That's all it takes to start.**
