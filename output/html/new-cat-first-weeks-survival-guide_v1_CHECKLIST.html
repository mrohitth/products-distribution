New Cat First 14 Days

Track your cat's adjustment progress day by day

Days 1-3: Setup

  • Sanctuary room identified (one room, not whole house)
  • Litter box placed away from food and water
  • One water station set up
  • Hiding spot created (box or carrier with blanket)
  • Feliway diffuser plugged in near hiding spot
  • Blinds and curtains closed in sanctuary room
  • No full-house access yet — sanctuary room only

Days 4-7: Routine Begins

  • Play session at 9:30pm (15 minutes, active play)
  • Final meal at 10:30pm
  • Lights out at 11:00pm
  • 30-minute rule in place: no response to nighttime crying
  • Cat eating consistently during the day
  • Litter box being used regularly

Days 8-14: Monitor Progress

  • Nighttime crying sessions shortening
  • Cat coming out of hiding more often
  • Daytime play sessions happening
  • Cat approaching you or staying in same room
  • Gradual room expansion started (one room at a time)
Red flags requiring vet visit (do not wait):
straining to urinate · not eating 24+ hours · hiding 3+ days straight · diarrhea/vomiting past 24 hours

Nighttime Routine Reference

Schedule

  • 9:30pm — Play session (15 min)
  • 10:30pm — Final meal of the day
  • 11:00pm — Lights out
  • 11:00-11:30pm — Expect crying (ignore)

What to Ignore

  • Meowing after lights out
  • Pawing at door or carrier
  • Scratching at walls
  • Any attention-seeking behavior at night
Reminder: Consistency beats intensity. Same sequence every night. Routine is the intervention.

Signs of Good Progress

  • Nighttime crying sessions shortened (30 min or less)
  • Eating regularly during the day
  • Coming out of hiding when you're in the room
  • Litter box usage consistent
  • Voluntary social contact increasing
