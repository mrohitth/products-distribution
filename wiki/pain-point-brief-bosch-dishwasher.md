## Common Failure Points

### Drain Pump / Impeller — The #1 Drain Issue

**Symptoms:**
- Water pooled at bottom after cycle.
- "E24" error code on display (most common Bosch drain error).
- Grinding or humming noise during drain cycle.
- Cycle stalls mid-way (can't drain → can't proceed to dry).

**Root Causes (cost rising):**
| Cause | DIY Difficulty | Part Cost | Notes |
|-------|---------------|-----------|-------|
| Clogged filter / sump | 🟢 Easy | $0 | Food debris, broken glass, label shreds block the sump |
| Drain pump impeller jammed | 🟡 Moderate | $0–$30 | Small object (olive pit, plastic clip) stuck in impeller |
| Drain pump failed | 🟡 Moderate | $40–$80 | Pump motor burned out or seized |
| Drain hose kinked/clogged | 🟢 Easy | $0–$15 | Under-sink hose crimped or grease-clogged |
| Check valve stuck | 🟡 Moderate | $10–$25 | Flapper stuck closed, water can't push through |
| Control board / wiring | 🔴 Hard | $100–$200 | Rare but possible — don't jump here first |

## Technical Complexity Signals

### What Scares DIYers About Bosch Specifically

1. **The "German Engineering" Factor** — Owners assume Bosch is too complex to touch. Reality: the drain pump is remarkably accessible on most models (bottom panel, 4–6 screws, unclip wiring).
2. **No User-Accessible Drain Pump Filter on Some Models** — Unlike many brands, some Bosch models lack a clean-out trap at the bottom. Debris reaches the pump directly.
3. **E24 Error Code Panic** — An error code with no obvious fix triggers immediate call-out reflex.
4. **The Triple Filter System** — Bosch's fine-mesh filters are excellent at catching debris, but owners don't clean them frequently enough, creating the illusion of a pump failure.
5. **Inverter Motor Quietness** — Bosch dishwashers are so quiet that owners can't hear if the pump is running, making diagnosis harder.

### Diagnostic Flow (Least Invasive First)

```
1. Clean triple filter system (under bottom arm)
2. Remove drain pump cover (accessible from inside tub on most models)
3. Spin impeller manually — does it move freely?
4. Check drain hose for kinks under sink
5. Test drain pump with multimeter (ohm reading)
6. Replace drain pump assembly
```

## Emotional Hook Angles (Cost of Call-Out vs DIY Confidence)

| Scenario | Call-Out Cost | DIY Cost | Savings | Emotional Trigger |
|----------|-------------|---------|---------|------------------|
| "Dishwasher won't drain" | $150 trip + $80 diagnostic + $XX parts | $15–$80 | **$150–$400** | "Is this a 5-minute fix I'm paying $300 for?" |
| "E24 error — terrified" | $150 + $80 | $0 (clean filter) | **$230** | "I literally just needed to twist a knob" |
| "Drain pump burned out" | $150 + $80 + $80 pump | $40 pump | **$270** | "I could swap this myself in 20 minutes" |
| "Something stuck in impeller" | $150 + $80 | $0 | **$230** | "A grape stem cost me $230??" |

### Core Emotional Fears

1. **"I'll make it worse"** — Owners fear they'll flood the kitchen or break something expensive by poking around.
2. **"I don't know what part to buy"** — Bosch uses model-specific part numbers. Owners freeze at the parts lookup stage.
3. **"I can't tell if the pump is bad or just blocked"** — This uncertainty is the single biggest driver of unnecessary service calls.
4. **"The error code is cryptic"** — E24 looks like a death sentence. It's almost always a simple blockage.
